# Temper Summit 2026 — Workshop Overview

## Title
**"Agentic AI at Work: Connect, Automate, Stay in Control"**

## Core Thesis
AI automation is only as good as the data it can access. The real unlock isn't just "use AI" — it's connecting AI to the tools we already use (Notion, Jira, Google Workspace, Mixpanel), designing workflows with human checkpoints, and building structured context that makes automation sustainable and expandable over time.

## Three Pillars of the Presentation

### 1. The Data Layer — Connections
AI needs access to your data to be useful beyond copy-paste. MCP (Model Context Protocol) is the emerging standard that lets AI tools connect to Notion, Jira, Asana, Google Workspace, and more — simultaneously. This is what makes the difference between "chatbot" and "agent."

### 2. Human in the Loop
Fully autonomous AI is risky. The right approach is semi-autonomous: AI does the heavy lifting, but humans approve, review, or redirect at critical checkpoints. This builds trust and prevents costly mistakes.

### 3. Context Engineering
The quality of AI output depends entirely on the context you feed it. Context engineering is the discipline of structuring, maintaining, and growing the information AI has access to — so your automations don't just work today, but keep working and improving over time.

## Tools We'll Reference
- **Gemini** (web app + CLI) — company-available AI
- **Claude Code** — terminal-based agentic AI with MCP connections
- **Cursor** — AI-powered code editor (same agentic concept, IDE context)
- **Google Workspace Studio** — no-code AI agents within Workspace
- **MCP** — the protocol that connects AI to all your tools
